April 23, 1919.

Dear Sam:

I received your letter and was very glad to hear from you. I am getting along fine and hope this letter will find you the same. I wonder who will smile first when we meet each other oh joy. I wish I was home now and join the bunch and have a good time. Yes they must be having a good time at home wish we was there too. Oh you made me think of the time when we used to sit in the dances at near Bay oh joy days of real sports. Will I think that we will dance together again some of these days coming oh joy. Just think of the day when we will meet again at dear old near Bay. That's what I say we should worry if everybody is getting married I don't care as long as I am not them ha ha. Everybody is doing it now they are smiles that makes us happy. They is a long long night awaiting into the land of our dreams oh joy when the time comes when I'll be on my way home.
on S.S. utopia I'll be glad to go home. It isn't very far from now till the school is out. So you are going to psyke sometimes you must write to me when you get to sythe. I'll be glad to see you again. So I wonder whose going to smile first. I believe I will smile first.

Oh I am sorry that I haven't got any picture of mine and will have some later on I am going to take my sister first and when I get some I'll send one to you then.

Why did you leave me today I think I know why ha ha. I had a good reason too so I left home know one knows. I didn't want to stay home and they was some thing else too oh joy. I'll tell you some other time when we meet again.

I should say not I won't forget my honey lunch never will forget you. I am glad that doctor woods is gone ha ha they got rid of him at last.

I received a letter from william parker not long ago and he said that they have dances every other night and base ball every Sunday afternoon oh joy I wish I was there for the good old times.

and he also said that they have a good agent he said that he is Indian he must be good. I have been in cushman box
8 month now I came September the 5th when I left home oh boy I have been away from home for a long time. I do wish it would come back the same way as it was last summer may be little better then it was hope we have good time again. Remember the times when we used to go out stoling enjoy my brothers Ernest and Walter and me we were plaining to have a big picknick this summer coming when I get home. Oh gee I hated to leave home when Eva and I had a farewell dance the night before we left home we left home Thursday night oh boy. Oh please send me your pictures in return letter I'll be glad to see your picture if you only send it honey.

Oh I wish the day would come now and we would be dancing around the floor once more. Not not every other night oh joy when we will be in the lunch.

I think all the boys are home now Walter and Ernest went home just few days ago they came to see me before they went home. Ernest and Walter they are coming after me when the school closes and papa said he was coming after me he came to see me once.

I don't know if I'll come back again next year hard telling I don't know what will happen this summer coming oh joy.

I might come back if I feel like coming back again.
Oh yes they will see me the first time when we come and I am the only one staying here I don't care.

It was Ruth Alabaster and Ida A. and Eva and me and Ellen Barker and Addline Williams and Iva Tyler and Martha Irving and William Parker and Lucy Smith and Harry McCarthy and Everett Green Allen & Tucker Friedlie Woostoff that was all and they all ran away. I am the only one now sometimes I get love some for them. I got letters from them nearly every mail day.

Oh I don't care as long as I'll be home for Fourth of July and my Birthday oh boy.

Oh it's just like summer now days makes me think of home. I got love some for you. I may be gone for a short short time. I wasn't going home for a while and I thought I would go home this summer. I am going home just for your sake.

Forget-me-nots

I must come in close dearie with best regards

From your loving Josyphine Hunter (answer soon as possible)

I Good by wish you good luck.

(Good by) (Think of me.)
Josephine H. Hunter

SPECIAL NOTE.
This form must be executed in duplicate when a child is transferred from a reservation to a nonreservation school. The Superintendent of the nonreservation school will retain the original for his files, and the duplicate shall be deposited in the reservation school records. The reservation superintendent should send to the Commissioner of Indian Affairs his certificate as provided by law. All the blanks must be properly filled in every case.
If the information called for on any part of the blank is not known, that fact should be stated. No space should be left unfilled. Whether the parents are living or dead, their names must be given.
The person who signs the blank as consenting to the transfer should indicate his relation to the applicant by marking out the word "parent," "guardian," or "next of kin," leaving unmarked only the title appropriate to the signer.
INDORSEMENTS.

Applicable laws relating to the transfer of Indian children from reservations and schools are as follows:

That hereafter no Indian child shall be sent from any Indian reservation to a school beyond the State or Territory in which said reservation is situated without the voluntary consent of the father or mother of such child if either of them are living, and if neither of them are living without the voluntary consent of the next of kin of such child. Such consent shall be made before the agent of the reservation, and he shall send to the Commissioner of Indian Affairs his certificate that such consent has been voluntarily given before such child shall be removed from such reservation. And it shall be unlawful for any Indian agent or other employee of the Government to induce, or seek to induce, by withholding rations or by other improper means, the parents or next of kin of any Indian to consent to the removal of any Indian child beyond the limits of any reservation. (28 Stats., p. 906.)

Provided, That hereafter no Indian child shall be taken from any school in any State or Territory to a school in any other State against its will or without the written consent of its parents. (29 Stats., p. 348.)

That no Indian pupil under the age of fourteen years shall be transported at Government expense to any Indian school beyond the limits of the State or Territory in which the parents of such child reside or of the adjoining State or Territory. (35 Stat. L., 781.)

The rules provide that—

A pupil who has been regularly enrolled in a nonreservation school must not be taken to any other nonreservation school without the consent of both superintendents and the Commissioner of Indian Affairs, and superintendents will be held to strict accountability for such pupils taken to their schools.

An Indian boy or girl 18 years old and over may, without the consent of parents or others, personally sign the application form on its being changed to suit the case; but in all cases where the parents are living they should first be consulted.

Compliance must be given to all orders and instructions promulgated in connection with the new course of study issued December 1, 1915.

The information as to public school facilities called for in the certificate of the Superintendent (third page hereof) must be given, and all superintendents are especially directed to exhaust every means to secure enrollment of Indian children in the State public schools, pursuant to instructions heretofore issued in this regard.

Physicians in the field should use this form to record the examination of pupils for transfer to nonreservation schools. It should accompany the pupils' transfer blanks.

The reverse side is intended as a card-index case-record for use by all Service physicians.
