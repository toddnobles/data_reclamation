Tacoma, Wash. Sept. 9, 1918.

Supt. O. C. Upchurch,
Spokane Agency,
Wellpinit, Wash.

Dear Mr. Upchurch:

I have recently had a letter from Mrs. Jennie Mason of Inchelium, Wash., requesting transportation for her two sons Peter and Delvin Hall to return to the Cushman School to complete their enrollment. They were enrolled in Sept. 1916.

The mother not having made the usual deposit for the return fare of the boys, and our school funds being very limited, I am unable to place the transportation requested, although the boys certainly ought to be in school and I am anxious for the boys to return.

If they or their mother have any funds which could be placed to the disposal of the mother at this time with which to return the boys, I will thank you for your efforts in that direction. Thanking you for an early reply, I am,

Yours very sincerely,

Superintendent
Cushman Indian School,
Tacoma, Wash. Sept. 9, 1918.

Mrs. Jennie Mason,
Inchelium, Wash.

Dear Friend:

In reply to your letter of August 26th regarding the return of your two sons Peter and Delvin Hall, would advise that there are no funds on deposit here for their fare back to Cushman. They will have to return at personal expense.

School started today and the boys should return as soon as they can get here so that they may not miss much school work for they can not afford to get behind in their class work else they will be at a disadvantage all the year.

Hope to see the boys soon, I am,

Yours very sincerely,

Superintendent

P.S. I have today written your agent Mr. Upchurch as he may be able to assist you as regards the tickets.
Aug 26 /18
exclusively,
Wasls.

Dear sir,

Will drop you a few lines to ask you if you will please send the boys their pass Pete and his little brother would like to leave it so as they can start from Spokane Sep. th sixth. Will you let me know right away please.

And Oblige

Jennie Mason.
