Mora, Wash., Jan. 18, 1919.

Mr. E. H. Hammond, Supt.
Cushman Trade School,
Tacoma, Wash.

Dear Sir:

Herewith are enclosed letters for Amy Jackson. They were placed in my care at the postoffice by the postmistress at Mora.

I would advise that these letters be opened by yourself or the matron in charge at her quarters, as the character of the letters can then be determined, whether the letters are for pure friendship, that they may be for a higher ideal or worthy of delivery to her.

Amy is inclined to be weak morally and by strict discipline her morals may be changed, with surroundings foreign from an Indian village, such as found in a boarding or training school. Her guardians (step-father and a deaf mother) lack control and discipline over her. Her home surroundings are not elevating. She escapes at her will from home into questionable company.

It is my advice that she be kept in school during vacation. She roamed here and there last summer without any escort or chaperone, giving Doctor Woods, (superintendent), myself and our police much trouble and annoyance.

At different times during her stay here and at Neah Bay for her vacation from Cushman, young men approached us for consent to marry her and being too young they were advised not to annoy as she was not of age yet.

I hope that you will not take this as an intrusion upon Amy's character, on your time or affairs.

Yours truly,

H.T. Markeshtum,
Teacher

SPECIAL NOTE.
This form must be executed in duplicate when a child is transferred from a reservation to a nonreservation school. The Superintendent of the nonreservation school will retain the original for his files, and the duplicate shall be deposited in the reservation school records. The reservation superintendent should send to the Commissioner of Indian Affairs his certificate as provided by law. All the blank spaces in the present form must be filled in. If the information called for on any part of the blank is not known, that fact should be stated. No space should be left unfiled. Whenever the parents are living or dead, their names must be given.
The person who signs the blank as consenting to the transfer should indicate his relation to the applicant by marking out the word "parent," "guardian," or "next of kin," leaving unmarked only the title appropriate to the signer.
The enrollment of Indian pupils in nonreservation schools is governed by the "Rules for the Indian Service, 1913," sections 12, 13, 14, 15, 18, 19, 36, 112, 113, 114, 117, 118, 124 (b).

The Rules should be consulted before filling out the application blanks.
