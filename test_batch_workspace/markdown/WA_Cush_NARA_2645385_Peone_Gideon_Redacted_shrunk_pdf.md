November 25, 1919

E. H. Hammond, Superintendent,
Cushman Indian School, Tacoma.

My dear Mr. Hammond;-

I note your letter of November 21st, regarding Gideon Peone, and that you have given him permission to return home to attend public school as soon as funds for his transportation are received.

In accordance with your recommendation, I am forwarding check for $30.00 from this boy's funds, but in sending this money, I would advise that I doubt very much if Gideon will attend school, should he return to his home. I believe it is best for this boy, if his health permits, that he remain where he is now. Gideon's parents live in the Village of St. Ignatius, on this reservation, where there is a good public school, but when Gideon is at home, instead of attending school, he spends most of his time loafing, when he is not driving his father's automobile around the country, and I have no reason to believe that should he return at this time, he will not resume his former practices, but if you think it is best for the boy to return to the reservation, I leave the matter in your hands.

Very truly yours,

[signature] Theodore Sharp  
Superintendent

Tacoma, Wash., Dec. 11, 1919

My. Theodore Sharp, Supt.
Fladhead Indian Agency
Dixon, Montana

My dear Mr. Sharp:

This afternoon I purchased a ticket for Gideon to his home town, Ravalli.

Gideon is very much interested in engineering and I was in hopes that his interest would induce him to remain in school until the end of the year, but the necessary restraint of a boarding school was too irksome and he was dissatisfied and claimed that he wished to return home to attend the high school at his home town or at Polson. Finally I decided that this school would be much better off without his eternal grouch which not only interfered with his own work, but was a rallying point for discontent among his intimates.

Very truly yours,

Superintendent.

EXTRACTS FROM SCHOOL RULES.

If the facilities of a school are not needed as provided in the above paragraph, children showing any Indian blood, whose parents live on an Indian reservation, and who if debarred from Government schools could not obtain an education, may be enrolled in the day and boarding schools of their reservation; these children, however, may not be transferred to nonreservation schools without special permission from the Commissioner of Indian Affairs.

Before enrolling a pupil superintendents must satisfy themselves whether he has left any other school without permission and whether he has been expelled for cause from any other school. A pupil who has been expelled for cause from any Indian school may be reenrolled in any Indian school only with permission of the Commissioner of Indian Affairs.

"The Secretary of the Interior may in his discretion withhold rations, clothing, and other annuities from Indian parents or guardians who refuse to or neglect to send or keep their children of proper school age in some school during a reasonable portion of each year." (27 Stat. L., 635. See 2 Dec. Comp., 308.)

Dear Mr. Peone:

Your son Gideon is not in jail, nor has he been for a long while. Because he deserted, taking with him a young boy, he was court-martialed and sentenced to sleep in jail a week, I believe it was.

I do not permit the Disciplinarian to keep boys in jail depriving them of their schooling thereby.

Very truly yours,

Superintendent.

FARM ENGINEERING.

RECORD CARD—PREVOCATIONAL DIVISION.

Each boy should devote 10 weeks to the work of this department during the three prevocational years. When he has accomplished the work as outlined in the course of study in a satisfactory manner, a suitable rating should be recorded. Ratings must be based on a practical application as well as a knowledge of the principles involved.

Ratings should be excellent (E), good (G), fair (F). If pupil's work is not worthy of a rating of fair, no grade should be recorded. Under "Remarks" mention anything that indicates interest, conduct, and proficiency of the pupil.

This card should be filed in folder for record of pupil (5-197). When the pupil is transferred to another school a copy of this card should be sent to the officer in charge of the school to which he is transferred.
