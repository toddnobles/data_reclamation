Dear Mr. Peel;

Please advise me the condition of Harry McDonald. His father says that the boy is sick and should be permitted to return home. I told him that I would not make recommendation without ascertaining the facts from you.

Very truly yours,

[signature]
Superintendent.

RS:AJ
Cushman Indian School  
Tacoma, Wash., Feb. 9, 1920

Mr. Fred C. Morgan, Supt.  
Colville Indian Agency  
Nespelem, Washington

Dear Mr. Morgan:

Replying to your letter of February 6, 1920, you are advised that at the present time Harry McDonald is confined in the school hospital with Measles, but is getting along nicely, the school nurse informs me. Aside from having the measles, Harry has been enjoying good health and it is not thought necessary to send him home on account of ill health.

Very respectfully,

Special Agent in Charge.
