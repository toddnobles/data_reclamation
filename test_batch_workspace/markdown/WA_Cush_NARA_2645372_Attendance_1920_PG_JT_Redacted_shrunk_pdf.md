1. The number of days school is in session corresponds in boarding schools to the number of days in the quarter, except in the quarters during which school is in vacation, when the average attendance for the actual number of days school is in session shall be regarded as the average attendance for that quarter. In day schools the number corresponds to the number of days in the quarter, less Saturdays, Sundays, and authorized holidays.
2. For the first quarter of the year this inquiry will be blank. For each of the last three quarters this entry will agree with item 8 of the report for the preceding quarter.
3. For the first quarter of the fiscal year this should include all pupils in attendance during any portion of the quarter when school was actually in session. If pupils are carried on the rolls during vacation periods and are dropped before the session opens they should not be included. For the last three quarters of the year report on this line only the new enrollments during the quarter, not including pupils reenrolled.
4. The pupils dropped during preceding quarters and who have been reenrolled must be here reported.
5. This inquiry should show the actual number of pupils in school during any portion of the quarter, and will agree with the total number of names entered in column 2 of the preceding pages. The figures here shown should be equal to the sum of the entries on lines 2, 3, and 4.
6. The number of pupils dropped during the quarter should be here reported.
7. Enter here the number of pupils withdrawn during the quarter who have returned before the end thereof.
8. Deduct the difference between entries on lines 6 and 7 from the total on line 5; the result will be the number of pupils on the rolls at the close of the quarter.
9. The total enrollment is the entire number of all pupils carried on the rolls of the school during the quarter. This item should agree with the entry on line 5.
10. The average attendance is obtained by dividing the total number of days in attendance by the number of days school was in session. This must be calculated correctly and carried to one decimal place.

* The names of pupils dropped from the rolls during the quarter must be entered here in alphabetical order, grouped separately by sexes. The names of all pupils here recorded must also appear on the preceding pages, in their proper places, in order that the school may receive credit for the number of days they were in attendance.
If a pupil is absent and returns during the same quarter the name should not be included, as he is not considered "dropped." Pupils who remain at the school during vacation and leave before the session actually opens should not be included. Make a separate report for such vacation pupils.

GENERAL INSTRUCTIONS.

This report is for all schools—boarding, day, Government, or contract, except public schools. It must be prepared and forwarded to the Indian Office IMMEDIATELY after the close of each quarter. A duplicate will be retained at the school or agency.

The names of all boys must be grouped at the beginning of the report in alphabetical order, and then followed by the girls similarly grouped. The information called for under each heading must be supplied in every case. If for any exhibit there is nothing to report, the word "None" should be substituted.

The principal of each school must prepare this report and sign the "Teacher's Certificate." During vacation or temporary suspension of a school, reports must be made stating the fact.

The reports for the quarters ending September 30 and June 30 should give the number of pupils who remained at the school during the vacation period on separate sheet No. 5-246-a, so that the record will agree with accounts papers in the matter of issuing rations to pupils. Rations will be issued to the number of pupils in daily attendance as shown upon this report, less attendance credited on the report of out-pupils.

Whenever required the teacher's certificate must be executed.

The superintendent must carefully examine this report and see that it is a correct statement of pupils and attendance, and forward one copy to the Commissioner of Indian Affairs.
