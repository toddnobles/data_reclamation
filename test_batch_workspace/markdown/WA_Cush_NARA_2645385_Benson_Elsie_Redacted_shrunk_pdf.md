Mr. Ben Benson,

Dear Sir:

Replying to your letter of May 2, 1920, in regard to your daughter, I have to inform you that this school closed permanently on May 14th. Elsie was sent to Mrs. Lunden at Astoria, Oregon.

If you wish to place her in another Indian school, you should make application for her enrollment in Chemawa Indian School, Chemawa, Oregon. Mr. Harwood Hall is the Superintendent of Chemawa School.

Yours respectfully,

Clerk in Charge.
Chignik, Alaska, May 2, 1920

Superintendent Cushman School,
Tacoma, Wash.,

Dear sir:

On her way to Chignik, Alaska, Mrs. Osmond called at the school to see my daughter and was informed that the school would close in June on account of not sufficient funds. If this is correct, I would like to have you send my daughter to Mrs. Lundine, 385 Twenty seventh street, Astoria, Oregon. In any event, I would like to have her sent there for her vacation.

When I sent her to the Cushman School I signed for three years, which time will expire next October. If possible, I would like my daughter to remain for three years longer under the same conditions as before. I can not very well afford to pay for this as I am now paying for two other children here in Alaska.

In the event of the school remaining open, I would appreciate it very much if you would notify me. Thanking you in advance, I am,

Very truly yours,

Ben Benson

VACATION REQUEST FOR PUPIL.

We, the undersigned, respectfully request that leave of absence be granted

Elsie Brown

from June 1st to October 1st to enable her to visit her home. We hereby agree to pay all expenses, including round-trip railroad fares, sleeping-car fares, meals, lodging, stage fares, telegraph and telephone messages, and other incidental expenses connected with the visit. We further agree that said pupil shall be returned to the Cushman Trade school on or before the last day of the leave of absence. We further agree to deposit with the superintendent of the school sufficient money to defray all expenses to be incurred, and should the pupils, or any one of them, fail to return to school at such time as may be agreed upon by the parents or guardian and the superintendent of the school in which the pupils are enrolled, the amount of money remaining with the superintendent, necessary to the return transportation of the pupil or pupils so neglecting to return, shall be forfeited to the Government; provided, however, that in all cases where it can be shown that there were justifiable reasons for the failure of the return of the pupil the forfeiture of the funds shall not take place.

If the pupil neglecting to return is from a reservation, the superintendent of the reservation shall certify to the reasons for the nonreturn of the pupil or pupils. If a pupil's home is not on a reservation, then the certificate of some reputable, disinterested citizen of the community may be requested as to the reasons for the nonreturn of the pupil or pupils.

Dear Sir:

Your letter of May 16th regarding my sister-in-law Elsie Brown has been received. As she is to stay with us this summer, we are taking the liberty of signing for her vacation.

Her father has left her with her Aunt quite while and the usual method of the older folks are to let the child work all summer and take most of the earnings. We therefore hope by having her stay with us, when she
leaves for school this fall to have it all to her credit. I am enclosing $13. to make up for what she has drawn of the $33.00 necessary as a deposit.

We will see that she returns this fall as we expect to go South.

I think she will have to go to Juneau and then take the weekly mail boat from here to Hoonah. The mail boat fare is $5.00.

Thanking you for your kindness in this matter. Mr. Hawksworth, Supt. of Alaska Native Schools are a personal friend of Mine.

H. Pkg. G. Henry Moy Hoonah, Alaska
